Exploring Participation Imbalance in Group Messaging

A. Gupta, J. Huang, K. Mangutov, C. Mathew

The power law is a relationship in which a relative change in one quantity results in a proportional relative change in the other quantity. An example of this is the Pareto principle, which suggests that in many events 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes. It can be observed in a wide range of phenomena ranging from software development to wealth inequality.

The aim of this visualization is to explore this principle as it applies to text group communication, namely GroupMe. The vertical black bar represents the point at which members have contributed 80% of the messages in the group.

Our data is compiled from fourteen of our own messaging groups. All data is anonymized.

We hypothesize that as group size increases, the dominance of certain individuals will decrease.

The visualization seems to confirm our hypothesis. As group size decreases, we see an increase in the equality of group participation.
